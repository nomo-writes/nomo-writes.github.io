Embassy terminology

Did you know that "embassy" isn't the only word we use to label that sacred place that is another country's little spot in our country?Â 

(1) Embassies and high commissions operate in capital cities

(2) High commission = embassy in Commonwealth countries

(3) There's three categories with "consulate" in the name:

  • Consulate
  • Consulate-General
  • Consulate headed by an Honorary Consul

And all three are embassies in non-capital cities.

(4) Honorary consuls are private businesspersons assisting in limited, part-time capacities only

These are not just made-up terms from Australia, they're in the Vienna Convention treaties, specifically on Diplomatic Relations and Consular Relations.

Source:
DFAT, "Roles and responsibilities of embassies and other representative offices" https://www.dfat.gov.au/about-us/department/Pages/roles-and-responsibilities-of-embassies-and-other-representative-offices

I found it really interesting to scroll through the huge lists that DFAT maintains of all our international relations in the form of real-life physical offices. And because "country" isn't used anywhere, you can see an official mention of Taiwain. As a layman, the seemingly random inclusion on this area but not another area in government will always amuse me a little.
